Identity and values exploration

below is a lot writing i did to try and explore and find out what my identity and values are, Its pretty unorganized and not all of it is useful, but it was a good excercise in trying to look at myself and what makes me 'me'

19 January 2024

my ramblings on my key values

friendship/kindness - one of the things that got me through a lot of the hardest things in my life was having true friends that were there not just when i was a fun person to be around but when i sucked. After having that for myself i always do the same and will I’ve seen how much just being a little kind to someone can affect their life, and most of the time its so easy to be kind rather than harsh.

fairness& acceptance - treating all people the same is a big thing for me, and came up often in online quizzes. I really dislike the idea of bias affecting someones judgments, and showing preferential treatment because of outside factors or existing connections. I strongly believe as humans we need to accept people for their differences and treat everyone with the same level of respect and love you would expect.

forgiveness - humans make a lot of mistakes and i believe you can learn, move on and be better through forgiveness. I dont mean this in a christian sense but in the sense that if someone has wronged me, i’ll always give them the chance to talk and make it better rather than writing them off and moving on. Some of the best friends and lessons ive learnt have came from this

family - in the last few years I’ve become much more close with my family, including extended. it made me feel almost guilty how i had not been there for most of my life. now i can say family is one of the first things that come to mind when i think of my values, i will always be there when my family needs me and wouldnt feel right not being able to be in touch with them. They give me a sense of safety by knowing that no matter what happens I will always have my family to love and support me.

my ramblings on my identity

the biggest identity former for me is my family. The traits they demonstrated in my childhood have definatly rubbed off on me, the most noticable being acceptance. both but in particular my father was made to pursue the career theyre parents wanted, and it really messed with him for a long time, so when they had children of their own it was really important to be accepting and encouraging of what they want to do and not try to lead or be in control of their childrens life, and this really rubbed off on me; I’ll always accept people for who they are and what they want to do (as long as their not hurting anyone)

a lot of my identity comes from my recovery from depression, one of the first things i had to do was be open and talk about everything, which was very hard as i was the type to bottle things up and keep the anger and frustration all to myself. But by being open it really started all the positive stuff happening and it felt really good to let it out, so now im definatly a very open person and dont feel shy about sharing personal things or things that might make me look weak or dumb.

one of the reccuring things people said about me is im one of the best people to be around, and always will make them laugh and feel included and wanted socially, so i believe another key things about my identity is radical inclusion - meaning that if you want to be involved or around something then to always try to include them and give them a go. I was always a naturally shy person and when i first started going to festivals (kiwiburn primarly) one of the key tenants was “radical self-inclusion”. Because of this i was invited into so many cool circles of people and activities i would have never had the courage to step into myself, but it has been so good for me.

Here is where I answered the questions from the previous sprint

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

one situation i found very hard was having to ask a good friend to move out of our flat, because they were really disruptive of other flatmates, messy, and was bad at paying bills on time. I felt very conflicted because this was a person i had a long history with and had vouched for, but things were clearly not working. i had to weigh up offending or possibly losing a close friend, and making my home life extremely uncomfortable. the decision i made was to ask the flatmate to leave, because i knew it was the correct decision; my flatmate wasnt in a good place and was causing harm to others, and by asking him to leave i knew he would return to his family home which would be a way to potentially get healthier. If i allowed him to stay he most likely would have kept up his negative behaviour.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Maori culture has begun to influence me greatly in the last few years, especially the emphasis on family and extended community. I had never been someone to be connected to people e.g i never introduced myself to my neighbors. But thru seeing how happy and connected, and how much it helps with day to day life i really strive to live like this and be more connected to those around me.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I Think my biggest strength when it comes to learning is my creativity & teamwork. I've always been a creative person who finds novel ways to conceptualise things and this comes in handy with coding, having to constantly learn new things that can be challenging to understand. I'm a big team player and often happier to be a great team member than a leader, and I believe this will be a big asset during study. Not only with working with other students and being able to support them, but since I'm great at following it will come in handy with following my tutors and the curriculim.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

the biggest limitation i have is my depressive mindset; i hate to mention depression so much but its really shaped so much of my life in the last 8 years or so. I still struggle with a very self-defeating mindset, and can tend to give up on something before even trying, and slowly spiralling that into thinking really negatively about myself. I can see it being a potential problem that when im working on a problem that im very stuck on, instead of asking for help (for fear of looking dumb) i will just retreat and hide to myself, and tell myself i shouldnt be doing this course or i made the wrong decision trying to return to coding. I know now this is the wrong mindset but it still pesters me sometimes especially in emotionally draining moments. another limitation is my lack of self discipline, which i believe comes from my stagnant life over the last 6 years. I havnt had to wake up at a certain time, or be anywhere, and havnt had to do things i didnt want to. as a result of that my brain is very lazy and i need to work extra hard to make myself active.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I often avoid conflict so it was quite hard to think of an example for this, but one could be during my old job there was often tension around who got which shift. The tension around shifts was so much that the manager left them to be decided on by staff because anytime they assigned shifts the staff would be unhappy. One strategy I tried when we were delegating shifts would be to identify the "best" shifts and give one to each member, and the "worst" shifts which I would try to get each member to take one. This is my attempt to be as fair as possible and is how I often try to resolve conflict. A lot of the time this was mostly effective, there would always be at least one person not happy with the outcome but I feel that is a lot less than if it was "first come first serve" Another strategy could be to rotate picking, so each week one person would get priority over picking their shifts. This could work well sometimes but as each person might have different commitments from week to week I think my strategy works best as it allows for more flexibility. If i was to go back in time and re-do this situation I would use a similiar strategy of being as fair as possible by evenly distributing the "good" and "bad" shifts, but maybe taking into consideration other things like how much money do people need to make that week etc

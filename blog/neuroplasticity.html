Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

understanding neuroplaticity can be a huge benefit for people trying to learn new things or change their habits. "rewiring" th brain is simply creating new synaptic connections, which it normally does, but by understanding neuroplasticity and the techniques that enhance it you can make these connections faster. You can also aid in healing from trauma, but I feel the best use of neuroplasticity for me personally will be to engage with and pick up the huge amount of skills being taught during EDA.

-Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit

the biggest thing I need to do to improve my brains neuroplasticity is exercise; I don't currently do much exercise and need to improve this. The other main principle that I can apply easier is meditation, I'm more experienced and have some a better grasp on applying meditation. One of the main principles of neuroplasticity is repetition, which I think will naturally happen during course but i will always keep this mind when revising code.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

exercise, good sleep, meditation, avoid comfortable repetition, and also forming new skills like languages or painting and drawing are a great help. It seems to be a core tenant to push your brain to have new experiences and repeat the actions of skills you want to retain.

Another way of increasing neuroplasticity is by doing your regular routine slightly different e.g brushing your teeth with the opposite hand, going a different direction on your commute or even switching hands for daily tasks can all help with working new neural pathways. Things like puzzles and chess are very good as well.

Discuss what growth mindset is and why it is relevant

growth mindset is a way of viewing things in that challenges and setbacks are not seen as limiting or bad, but as a growth potential and understanding that your skills are not set in stone. Someone with a fixed mindset would take criticism personally and take their struggles to heart, seeing themselves as dumb. It's relevant to take on a growth mindset as it will greatly help dealing with the amount of work we will encounter in EDA.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

Reminding myself that feeling overwhelmed with work is much better than being lazy and continuing with my non-challenging life is far worse than the duress I'm currently going through, to achieve a much better life.

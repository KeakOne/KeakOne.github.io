Learning plan

below are my answers to questions to formulate my learning plan.

20 January + 9 February 2024

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I'm hoping to get back into life and pick up from where i left off a few years ago. Apart from helping get my life in order I want to find employment somewhere I like and not just because they would hire me. Beyond that if I make any impact at Dev academy it would be nice to be an example that you can come back from big challenges in your life, and if things dont work out the first time it doesnt mean youve failed.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My view of my strengths and limitations has not changed much, I still believe my main strengths are social intelligence and teamwork, and my main limitations are motivation and managing my depressive tendancies. One other strength I've found through foundations is I have a good knowledge of coding and have been able to help some other students when we've been talking about our sprints or things we're stuck on in terms of talking through logic or trying to find the fault in their code, so I'm hoping i can become more helpful which will not only be handy to students, but also re-enforce skills and best of all make me a more employable person.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I mentioned it previously but managing my deppresive tendancies will be the hardest, because it drips into many factors of life. It could potentially make me "give up" and not try as hard as i can, or getting the most out of the EDA experience. I am slightly worried about this but I know I'm capable of doing this and coming out the other end fine now, but once it starts and im in the pressure of it all I could fall apart. I'm determined to not have this happen by keeping it present in my mind that by doing this and trying as hard as i can I will be taking steps to directly beat my deppresive tendancies and form a healthier life.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would love to improve my leadership/managerial skills first. I've always shyed away from being a leader and taking charge on projects or being the leader of a group, not because i wasnt able to but because I'm too shy or scared of the responsibility. The other skill I want to improve is my motivation, or maybe even more so my "hustle" - the ability to be excited to get out of bed early, and eager to approach challenges, and do everything possible to make things happen. I've been a very stagnant person for a long time so I'm hoping the demanding, fast-pace nature of bootcamp will spur this motivation out of me.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

My expectation are that the bootcamp team show true interest in helping students while its work hours. This means just being really present and valueing the students time and money as if it were their own. I think its important to make sure communication is really good as well so its clear what we are working on, what the goals are etc. Another important goal would be to have some understanding of individual students problems and be understanding, not in a hand-holding way but just recognising particular students are struggling and prioritising helping them if other students are ok to work by themselves.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I expect myself to step-up and be able to be in time for every class, and also to make an effort to be in campus as much as i can. I also expect myself to not coast if im comfortable with the work, but to take on the stretch challenges. I expect myself to not cave-in to my mind playing tricks and perservere through hard times, with the understanding that once I've got through I'll be stronger and better for it. I expect myself to keep my end-goal in mind, and even after bootcamp to keep the same work ethic in creating my portfolio, applying for jobs, coming up with cool personal projects for as long as it takes until I have that job I want.

Based on your knowledge of Bootcamp, how and where will you seek help in a timely way?

I'm going to commit to asking for help when i need it and have given an honest effort to something. I've held myself back in many aspects of life by not asking for help and retreating and staying silent so i'm not going to do it this time. I'm going to keep other students in mind and use my social awareness to make a call on if someone else requires more assistance than me, and be willing to wait for someone who is struggling more than myself. Managing the workload will be challenging but i believe by staying communicative with the tutors and putting in more than the minumum time requirements while being fully present will give me enough to complete the workload.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

I expect the facilitation team to honestly listen when I have a problem, and do their best to help. i dont expect to be personally guided to find answers but i do expect honest help that can lead me to the correct answers. Another expectation I have is that their will be understanding during times of extreme stress, although it might not happen to me it is nice to think that the staff will be able to help someone find a way through their struggles, and give them some steps to continue working.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things

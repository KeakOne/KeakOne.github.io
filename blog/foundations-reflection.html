Foundations reflection

This entry is about looking back on the last 5 weeks of foundations before starting bootcamp :)

8 February 2024

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

1 - The first thing that comes to mind is my ego holding me back in terms of asking for help. I've always been very embarrassed or hesitant to ask for help, but i didnt realise it was related to ego. I thought it was just embarrasment for embarrasment sake, but it was actually because i think of asking for help as a sign of being dumb or not meeting my own expectations which hurts my ego, even uknowingly.

2 - I learnt that my ego is a huge part of what has been holding me back, I put a lot of weight of expectations on myself and constantly compare myself to others or where i think I should be in my life at this point. I didnt really accept that self-expectations were a part of ego, but it ultimately is as its a projection of yourself or what you think of yourself. By always thinking I am doing a bad job by comparison I'm just holding myself down and reducing any accomplishments I've made.

3 - I learnt that the ego is not only just when you think you are better than other people, but Its something we also used to protect ourselves. Throughout my depression I always told myself I wasnt smart, motivated or lots of other negative things, but now framing it through ego I was doing this to try protect myself from having a go and maybe failing. My ego was working against me and making me not see things clearly, and telling me that its not worth trying because you'll fail anyway, so save yourself that pain and just dont try, which is ultimately what led to me stalling in life for so many years.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

All of these roles help you be a better, healthier coder and person but I think the main purpose is to make people work better together. By having empathy you can feel a lot more for your co-workers and be more aware of when theyre not feeling well or up to a task. By being self-aware you can manage how you interact with people to bring the best of yourself, and be aware of what works for you and what doesnt in a collaborative setting. You can use the knowledge you have of yourself to lay out the best road for yourself around others.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

What suprised me was so many of the things I've wanted to learn more about, or start practising in my own life was a part of the core curriculim. Things like growth mindset, mindfullness, meditation, neuroplasticity had all been talked about with me before during therapy and had been enjoyable when i had put them into practise, but I had not stayed vigilant. Seeing these aspects in the curriculim made me realise i need to continue practising and exploring these topics not just for the previous reasons but for now also being a healthy coder in the future.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The hardest part was reaching out and interacting with other people, like in the listening and looping excercise. I'm quite a reserved person so I find it hard to talk to people I've never met in real life before. It was also challenging to keep some of the ideas from core learning in my head when things are stressfull or I'm under a time constraint, I seem to just be less vigilant and not think of using the core practises at times like that.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I believe there is a heavy focus on core learning because the concepts taught will carry on well past our time at EDA. If there is research and feedback from employers saying these practises are what theyre looking for then thats a huge plus, but its also for the well being of the individual. Understanding your key values, having empathy, approaching challenges with a growth mindset are all things that will not only be great for someone in work but in their personal life, and help them cope with a lot of the common problems people encounter in tech employment.

I think the amount of time spent is well spent as its shown true that investment and time put into these practises, even just small amounts of time each day, will greatly improve a persons life overall.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead?

For me it didnt feel like a waste of time because I was seeking more than just coding skills with coming to EDA. personally I was looking for life skills and a way to bring myself back to a ready state to enter the work force after a long time being absent, so because of that core has been really important. However If i didnt have much pre-existing coding experience and was finding the curriculim extremely hard, and questioning if coding was right for me then I would feel it is a waste because I want to improve my coding skills so much that other things would feel like focusing on the wrong area.
